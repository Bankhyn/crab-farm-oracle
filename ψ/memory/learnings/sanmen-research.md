## 🏞 Farming Methods
```
Traditional Pond Farming:
  - Pond Size: 15m wide × 20m long × 1.5-2.0m depth
  - Stocking Density: 5-10 crabs/m²
  - Feed: Fresh fish, trash fish, mollusks
  - Water Exchange: 10-20% per week (tidal)
  - Challenges: Disease outbreaks, water quality management

Modern Cage Box System (Individual Boxes):
  - Box Size: 30×40×15 cm
  - Substrate: Fine sand (3-5 cm) if hard-shell
  - No substrate for soft-shell farming
  - Stocking: 1 crab per box
  - Water Flow: Continuous recirculation (RAS)
  - Advantages:
    - Prevents cannibalism (100% survival rate)
    - Better growth monitoring
    - Disease control
    - Space efficient (200-300 crabs/m² vs 5-10/m²)

Vertical Farming with RAS:
  - Structure: Multi-tier stacking systems
  - Tank Capacity: 1000-5000L
  - Individual Boxes: 20-200 per tier
  - Water Treatment: Complete RAS system
  - Components:
    - Mechanical filtration (sponge/sock/debris filter)
    - Biological filtration (bio-balls/ceramic media)
    - UV sterilization (optional)
    - Aeration (air stones/diffusers)
  - Benefits:
    - High density production
    - Space saving (vertical)
    - Year-round production possible
    - Controlled environment (indoor)
```

## 📚 Lessons for Thailand
```
From Sanmen's Success:
1. Start small, scale gradually
   - Sanmen started with traditional ponds
   - Evolved to cage box + RAS
   - MVP approach: 20 crabs in individual boxes

2. Focus on water quality
   - Water quality is foundation of success
   - Monitor closely (temp, salinity, pH, DO)
   - Filtration is essential

3. Prevent cannibalism
   - Individual boxes = key innovation
   - Prevents fighting and stress
   - Higher survival rates (85-95% reported)

4. Disease management
   - Proactive prevention better than reactive treatment
   - Biosecurity crucial
   - Regular health checks

5. Market focus
   - Soft-shell offers 4-5x price premium
   - Faster turnover (3-4 weeks vs 4-5 months)
   - Premium markets (restaurants, export)

6. Technology adoption
   - RAS systems proven at effective
   - Vertical farming increases capacity
   - Automation reduces labor costs
```
